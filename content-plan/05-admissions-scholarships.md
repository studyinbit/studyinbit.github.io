## Section 5: Preparation Guide
### Headline
"Start Preparing Now"

### Subheadline
"The Chinese math standard is high. Don't wait until you arrive."

### Academic Preparation (The "Iron Triangle")

#### 1. Advanced Mathematics
**Recommended Resources:**
- *Calculus: Early Transcendentals, 8th Edition* by James Stewart
- Focus: Limits, Derivatives, Integration
- Online: Khan Academy Calculus

**Why:** Chinese calculus courses move fast. Familiarity with concepts is crucial.

#### 2. University Physics
**Recommended Resources:**
- *University Physics* by Young & Freedman
- Focus: Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Electromagnetism
- Online: MIT OpenCourseWare Physics

**Why:** Physics courses assume strong foundation. Review now to stay ahead.

#### 3. Programming
**Recommended Resources:**
- *C Programming: A Modern Approach* by K.N. King
- Focus: Logic, Loops, Pointers, Data Structures
- Online: CS50 (Harvard's intro course)

**Why:** Programming courses start with C. Basic familiarity helps immensely.

### Language Preparation

#### Chinese (Mandarin)
**Target:** HSK 2-3 before arrival
**Resources:**
- Duolingo Chinese
- HelloChinese app
- YouTube: "Learn Chinese with Emma"

**Why:** Even in English-taught programs, lab language is often Chinese. Basic Mandarin helps with daily life.

#### English
**Target:** IELTS 6.5+ / TOEFL 90+
**Resources:**
- IELTS practice tests
- Academic writing courses
- Technical English vocabulary

**Why:** Lectures, textbooks, and exams are in English. Strong English = better grades.

### Cultural Preparation
- Watch videos about Chinese university life
- Learn basic Chinese etiquette
- Understand academic expectations
- Connect with current Indonesian students

## Section 7: FAQ
### Common Questions

**Q: Can I apply without CSCA?**
A: For 2026 admissions, CSCA is required for scholarship consideration. Check with admissions for non-scholarship applications.

**Q: When will I know if I got a scholarship?**
A: CSC scholarship results typically come in March-April. BIT scholarships are decided with admission.

**Q: Can I change my major after admission?**
A: Limited major changes possible after first year, subject to academic performance and department approval.

**Q: What if I don't get a scholarship?**
A: Self-funded study is possible. Total cost: ~60,000-80,000 RMB/year (tuition + living). Still very affordable compared to Western universities.

**Q: Is there an interview?**
A: Some scholarship applications may require interviews. You'll be notified if selected.
